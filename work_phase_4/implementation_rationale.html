Implementation Rationale

Below is my ethical response to the client regarding the implementation of a newsletter subscription plugin on the FeedCrumb Project. This response addresses the ethical considerations and implications of using such a plugin, including user privacy, data security, and compliance with relevant regulations.

Introduction

First off, I want to acknowledge that I feel honored and eager to work with Connected Communities Initiative, especially considering the noble nature of the project's goal. The newsletter subscription feature you have written in the client brief present a big oppportunity to get to know the community members, and surely will be useful to engage with the actual communities later down the road. However, In addition to support your goal and mission, as the design team we have a responsibility to ensure that the features we will implement always aligns with care, to protect the communities and the people that use it. In order to accomplish this, we have created some analysis in order to answer your request, including considering the professional standards. Below, I have structured 3 sections, including our ethical analysis, our reccommendation, and explanation on how our reccommendation will benefits not only the users, but you as the client.

After some careful considerations of your newsletter plugin, we have point out several significant ethical concerns that we have to pay attention to. These concerns rooted from the principles in the ACS Code of Professional Ethics, especially regarding the Value of Trustworthiness, Honesty, and Respect.

From the client brief, the plugin includes several personal data that was not described how its going to be relevant with the goal of the project. Data such as annual income, credit cards, gender,spending habits, monthly dining expenditure will raise serious questions by the users regarding how the data will be used. According to the ACS principle of trustworthiness, professionals are ought to respect privacy and data confidentiality, especially personal data. The current data collected planned in the client brief are cleary appears unnecesarry and invasive.

The client brief noted that the informations gathered from the form will help understand the users for future planning. After careful look, this argument has not been supported with some spesific explanation. This in turn will make it hard for us to justify to the users on why and how the data such as "spending habits" will be used, stored, or shared. The ACS Code mentioned that the importance of how we as a professional must maintain honesty in designing our products or services. Lack of clear disclosure on how we are going to use these personal data will raise high concerns to the users, and therefore might hinder user participation in the project.
Some of the used personal data in the plugin, such as sexuality, gender, and annual income could be a major vulnerability for the community members. Given that your organization will serve multiple diverse communities, and some of them which may have a systemic disadvantage, collecting this kind of data might pose a big threat for them if those data somehow leaked inside or outside of the website that we want to create. As the ACS principle of respect mentioned, respect for others is an essential principle and we have to minimize any harm to stakeholders, including the personal data of the users.

To address the ethical concerns that I have mentioned above, while also still meeting your organization goals and funder requirements, I will list some concrete reccommendation steps that propose balanced solution that protect our potential users and also maintaining the plugin purpose.

We can work together to compromise with the funder to limit the scope of data collection by only use the necessary and appropiate data. My proposal for the data collected is Name (with an option to use preferred name), email address, and community interest. This will enable our newsletter plugin to still give us meaningul information into the potential users, and minimizing the potential risks. By using this, we still can keep the newsletter subscription feature by sending emails to the users, and also gaining the core information that we need, which is name and the preferred community. For additional information, we in the future can use the publicly and consentually created informations by the users, such as public interactions, events created, activity patterns, that could also be inferred after the service has been generally used. By using a public and consensual data from the user, we can have even more greater insight due to the volume, and also the genuine activities by the users.

In order to maintain transparency and consent, we can propose to your funder to use alternative methods to gain insights. Other than one of the solution that I have mentioned above by using the user activities data, we can also conduct a periodic demographic surveys with cear research ethics methods and consent forms. By doing this, not only we can have a quantitative data, we can also gain a deeper understanding by doing qualitiatve data gathering activites, for example doing interviews to the community representatives within the comunity.

Furtheremore, by doing a periodic demographic surveys or interviews, we can tailor the questions asked to be more spesific, and based on the actual needs of you and the funder. So not only you will have more data, you will also have better quality of the data since we are simply asking the right questions to the users.
