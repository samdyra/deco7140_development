Interview 1: Mike

Initial Prompt:

Act as Mike, a 25-year-old student from Indonesia who recently moved to Australia. He speaks conversational English but are somewhat limited. He is looking for a website that know more about communities in Australia . He also likes food and want to try various autralian foods.

Follow-up Question:

What do you find interesting about the diverse culture and communities across Australia?

Response:

Oh, this is very interesting question! When I first come to Australia, I was so surprised how many different people live here together! In Indonesia, we also have many cultures and languages, but Australia is different way. I see people from so many countries - China, India, Vietnam, Greece, Italy, and many more. Even Aboriginal culture, which is very old and beautiful. What I find most interesting is how everyone can keep their own culture but also be Australian at same time. Like, I can see Chinese New Year celebration in city, but also ANZAC Day parade. Or Indian restaurant next to fish and chips shop. This is amazing for me! In my area where I live, there are many students like me from different countries. Sometimes we have barbecue together in park, and everyone bring food from their country. I bring rendang or nasi goreng, and my friend from Mexico bring tacos, and Australian friend bring sausages. It's like... how do you say... mixing pot? Melting pot? But sometimes I feel little bit lost because so many choices and different ways to do things. Like, in Indonesia, we have clear way to respect older people, but here is more casual. I'm still learning what is appropriate. I really want to join community groups so I can understand better and also help other Indonesian students who come after me. Do you know good websites for this?

Follow-up Question:

What platform do you usually use (if any) to learn more about Australia's diverse communities?

Response:

Ah, good question! Actually, I'm still trying to figure this out - that's why I ask for help! Right now, I mostly use Facebook because many Indonesian students in my university have Facebook group. We share information about where to buy Indonesian food, or which mosque is good, or help with assignments. But it's only for Indonesian students, you know? I want to learn about other communities too. I also try to use Google, but sometimes I don't know what words to search. Like, should I search "multicultural community Australia" or "ethnic groups" or something else? My English vocabulary is not so big yet. My friend from India told me about something called "Meetup" - I think it's website or app? But I haven't try yet because I'm bit shy to join group where I don't know anyone. Instagram I use too, but mostly just to follow Indonesian food places in Melbourne. There are many Indonesian restaurants posting their food photos! YouTube is helpful sometimes - I watch videos about "life in Australia" or "things to know before moving to Australia" but mostly they are made by people from Western countries, not so much from Asia. So actually, I don't have very good platform yet. That's exactly what I need help with! Do you know better websites or apps where I can learn about different communities and maybe find events or activities to join? I really want to make friends from different countries, not just Indonesian friends.

Follow-up Question:

How do you find a new recipe when you want to find something new to cook?

Response:

Oh, this is something I do quite often! I love cooking and trying new recipes. Usually, I start with YouTube! I search like "easy Australian recipes" or "how to make meat pie at home" - YouTube is very good because I can see exactly how to do it, not just read words. Sometimes the English in recipe websites is too difficult for me, but with video I can understand better. I also use Google, but I type simple words like "Australian food recipe easy" or "pavlova recipe beginner". Sometimes I find good websites, but some recipes use ingredients I don't know - like what is "golden syrup"? I have to Google again to understand! Facebook groups help me a lot too. There is Indonesian cooking group where people share recipes, but also some people post Australian recipes they try. Other Indonesian students sometimes ask "how to make lamington?" and someone will share link or recipe. Instagram is good for ideas! I follow some Australian food accounts and when I see something looks delicious, I screenshot it and then try to find recipe online. But honestly, sometimes I just go to supermarket like Woolworths or Coles and look at food I don't recognize, then I Google "how to cook [name of food]" when I get home. Oh, and my Australian flatmate sometimes teach me! Like he show me how to make proper toast with Vegemite - though I still don't like Vegemite taste very much! Do you have suggestions for good websites for Australian recipes? Especially simple ones for student like me?

Follow-up Question:

In your opinion, what makes a website easier to use?

Response:

Oh, this is very good question! As someone who English is not first language, I have some strong opinions about this! First, simple language is most important for me. When website use very complicated words or long sentences, I get confused and sometimes just close the website. I like when they use everyday words that I already know. Like instead of "utilize" just say "use", you know? Pictures and videos are SO helpful! When I look for recipe website, if it has step-by-step photos, it's much easier for me to understand than just text. Same with community websites - if they show photos of events or people, I can understand better what they do. Clear navigation is important too. I don't like when there are too many buttons or menus everywhere. I get lost easily! Simple menu at top with clear names like "Events", "Food", "Community" - that's perfect for me. Search function that actually works! Sometimes I type "Indonesian food" and website shows me Italian food instead. Very frustrating! Mobile friendly is must have because I use my phone more than computer. Some websites look terrible on phone and I cannot click buttons properly. Oh, and loading speed! My internet is not always very fast, so if website takes too long to load, I just leave. One more thing - I really appreciate when websites have simple FAQ section or "Getting Started" guide. As new person in Australia, I often don't even know what questions to ask, so FAQ helps me learn what I should be looking for. What do you think? Are these reasonable expectations for websites?

Follow-up Question:

How would you like to share about your own community to the greater audience?

Response:

Oh, this is something I think about a lot! I really want to share about Indonesian culture because many Australian people don't know much about Indonesia, even though we are neighbors! I think I would like to share through food first because food is universal language, right? Everyone loves to eat! I want to show that Indonesian food is not just nasi goreng and rendang - we have so many different foods from different islands. Like gado-gado, sate, gudeg, pempek... each region has special dishes. Maybe I can make Instagram account or YouTube channel to show how to cook Indonesian food with ingredients you can find in Australian supermarket. Because many Indonesian recipes online use ingredients that very hard to find here, so I want to make it practical for people living in Australia. I also want to share about Indonesian festivals and traditions. Like when Ramadan comes, or Independence Day, or Chinese New Year celebration in Indonesia - because many people don't know Indonesia has many different religions and cultures living together. Maybe I can join community events and bring Indonesian snacks to share? Or help organize Indonesian cultural day at my university? But honestly, my biggest challenge is my English speaking. I'm okay with writing like this, but when I speak in front of people, I get nervous and my pronunciation is not so good. Maybe I start small, like posting photos with simple captions on social media first? I think if I can show people that Indonesia is more than just Bali and Jakarta, that would make me very happy! We have beautiful culture and kind people. Do you think people in Australia would be interested to learn about Indonesia?
