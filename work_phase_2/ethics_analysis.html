Ethics Analysis

Ethical Risk 1: User data collection on the Newsletter Subscription Feature

ACS Value:

Honesty

Rationale:

"You will be honest in your representation of skills, knowledge, services and products."

Due to the functional requirement of this website that states the usage of a newsletter subscription feature that requires user data input, several precautions must be made regarding the act of collecting, managing, and sharing these personal data.

Solution:

One of the solution in order to overcome this is to be as transparent to the user as possible. Honesty and transparency to the user regarding data collection and management should be mandatory, This could be done by using several steps, including showing a clear and honest terms and conditions, having a feature to delete the user's own data, including their accounts, always shows prompts before any data collection activity occur, and make sure the website is still fully functional even in case the user do not intend to gave their permission for personal data collections.

Ethical Risk 2: Conflict of Interest for certain group

ACS Value:

The Primacy of the Public Interest

Rationale:

"You will place the interests of the public above those of personal, business or sectional interests."

Because the website created will host various cultures accross Australia, there will be risk of giving a certain preference to a certain groups, especially those who might have some commercial value. This can lead to bias in how each cultures represented.

Solution:

The website created will have to keep fair representation, without any bias, and guarantee all group of people and culture receive an equal chance and treatment, regardless of any economic interests or any other biases. In order to do this, a community representative should always be given the chance to advocate any mistreatment to the project's stakeholder. Furtheremore, a transparent decision-making for any changes created later down the road should be made mandatory, to maintain the website's integrity by preventing any potential public distrust from the users.

Ethical Risk 3: Social Harms such as Profanity, Online Harassment, and Disinformation

ACS Value:

The Enhancement of Quality of Life

Rationale:

"You will strive to enhance the quality of life of those affected by your work."

The risk of social harm will always exist in an social-media like softwares, as the website itself will encourage users to share their thoughts and ideas in any verbal communications. These expression, will also allow social harms, such as profanity and cyber bullying to occur in the website, and therefore risking the quality of life to those who affected.

Solution:

A proper content moderation system will be have to made to make sure we can minimize any social harms. Features such as community notes that is implemented in X (twitter) could also be implemented, so that human moderators trained in cultural sensitivity can assess any potential social harms transparently. Furthermore, a regular communication between the community and the stakeholder of this project can also help to speed up the feedback-improvement loop regarding any feature neccesarry to minimize social harms.
